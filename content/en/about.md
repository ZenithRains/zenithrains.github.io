I am pursuing a PhD in Applied Mathematics at Beihang University, working on incompressible fluid dynamics and chemotaxis systems. Outside mathematics, I enjoy mountaineering and calligraphy, and I play the erhu. I like sorting out complicated problems and explaining them in accurate, plain language.

I think it matters to have an independent place on the internet where I can write over time. Publishing my work subjects it to other people's reading, questions, and criticism. It also forces me to think more carefully about what I believe and how I understand the world around me.

I write about mathematics, research, interests, thoughts, and feelings here.

Large language models are advancing rapidly, but writing still matters. So do the learning, judgment, and organization it requires. I intend to keep writing.
