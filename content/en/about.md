I am currently pursuing a PhD in Applied Mathematics at Beihang University, where my research focuses on incompressible fluid dynamics and chemotaxis systems. Beyond mathematics, I have a wide range of interests, including mountaineering, calligraphy, and playing the erhu, a traditional Chinese musical instrument. Whether in research or everyday conversation, I enjoy making complex ideas easier to understand and explaining them as clearly, accurately, and plainly as I can.

I have long believed that it is important to have a place on the internet where one can speak in one's own voice and continue writing over time. Writing in public means that one's words must stand up to the reading, questioning, and scrutiny of others. It also encourages me to think more carefully about what I truly believe and how I understand the world around me.

This website, then, is more than a collection of mathematical writing and personal essays; it is also a way of introducing myself to the world. I hope that visitors will encounter not only my research and interests, but also something of my inner life—my values, sensibilities, and emotions.

At a time when large language models are advancing at remarkable speed, writing—and the capacity for continued learning, independent judgment, and coherent thought that writing cultivates—has become all the more important. I hope to use this space to document my reflections, explorations, and growth over the years.
